How to Play
◆ What Is This Game?

You are Stephen Wolfram, convinced the universe runs on cellular automata — not continuous equations. Academia disagrees. Fight through 4 stages of hostile academics, survive their attacks, and defend your theory long enough to shift the scientific paradigm. Sanity and Funding reset to 100 at the start of each new stage.

◆ Your Moves
Move What It Does Cost
Theorize
Compute Automata		 Adds +1.5x to your Evidence Multiplier. Stack this before attacking for big damage. You stay up all night running simulations. -10 Sanity
Present / Publish
Attack		 Deals 20 × Evidence Multiplier damage to the enemy's Dogma HP, then resets the multiplier to 1x. Submitting papers and conferences cost money. -15 Funding
Write Grant Proposal
Restore Funding		 Restores +30 Funding. Soul-crushing paperwork, but keeps the lights on. -15 Sanity
Touch Grass
Restore Sanity		 Restores +30 Sanity. A mental health day. The trees don't have opinions about discrete mathematics. -10 Funding
◆ How to Win
  • Use Theorize to build up your Evidence Multiplier, then Present / Publish to deal massive damage.
  • Defeat all 4 academic enemies by reducing their Dogma HP to 0.
  • Survive Stage 5 — the Sycophantic Committee won't attack, they just grovel.
  • Select Accept Nobel Prize to win the game.
⚠  Watch out: If Sanity or Funding hits 0, it's game over — no matter what stage you're on. Keep both topped up. Some enemies heal themselves or hit very hard.
